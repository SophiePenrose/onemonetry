# FINAL EMAIL GENERATION SPECIFICATION v7
## Information-Rich Sequence Design (First-Use Focus)

---

## OVERVIEW

This specification defines the email generation system for the Revolut Business mid-market prospecting tool. It is designed for **first-use** — producing the highest-quality, most personalised, voice-authentic outreach possible from day one, leveraging the comprehensive Research Dossier that the scoring engine produces for every prospect.

The system is built around one core paradigm shift: traditional cold outreach cadences are designed for **information scarcity** (multiple emails testing different angles because the SDR doesn't know what will land). This system is designed for **information abundance** — the dossier already identifies the right angle, so the sequence builds conviction on that angle rather than testing alternatives.

## PART 1: SOPHIE'S PROSPECTING TONE PROFILE

Calibrated from actual sent emails (Maples Group, Shire Leasing, Momenta Finance — Feb 2026).

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SOPHIE PENROSE — PROSPECTING TONE PROFILE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

OVERALL VOICE:
Professional, measured, and observational. The voice of someone 
who has done genuine research and is making a considered 
observation — not pitching. Written to be respected, not liked.

CORE PATTERNS:

1. RESEARCH-FORWARD OPENING
   Open by referencing something specific and recent. Interpret 
   the achievement strategically.
   Pattern: "[Company]'s recent [event] marks/highlights/signals 
   [strategic interpretation]..."

2. "USUALLY, WHEN..." TRANSITION (signature move)
   Bridge from the specific to the generalised pattern.
   "Usually, when a company reaches this stage..."

3. PAIN AS CONSEQUENCE OF SUCCESS
   Frame challenges as natural shifts that follow growth.
   "The challenge shifts from [old focus] to managing the 
   [new complexity]."

4. ABSTRACTED PAIN VOCABULARY
   - "treasury friction"
   - "operational friction"
   - "financial complexity"
   - "FX friction"
   - "operational treasury complexity"

5. REVOLUT AS UNIFIED PLATFORM
   - "a unified platform to bridge that gap"
   - "acts as a unified platform"
   - "supports this kind of strategic evolution"

6. SOFT, OPEN CTA (Sophie's standard variants)
   - "Would it be worth a call to discuss..."
   - "Would it make sense to discuss..."
   - "Would you be open to a call for us to discuss..."

7. SIGN-OFF
   "Best," or "Kind regards," + "Sophie Louise Penrose" (full name)

VOCABULARY YOU USE:
"continuing to scale", "scaling phase", "level of volume", 
"sophisticated", "definitive shift", "strategic evolution", 
"critical operational pillar", "critical priority", "high-volume", 
"cross-border", "intercompany", "aligns perfectly with", "marks 
a definitive shift", "remains a critical", "similar clients", 
"clients in the space"

VOCABULARY TO AVOID:
Casual: "I noticed", "I came across", "quick question", "just 
wanted to", "Hey"

AI tells: "delve", "navigate", "robust", "seamless", "tapestry", 
"in today's", "in the realm of", "stands as a testament", 
"speaks volumes", "it's worth noting that"

Buzzwords: "leverage" (verb), "synergy", "circle back", 
"deep dive", "low-hanging fruit"

Apologetic: "sorry to bother", "hope this finds you well", 
"hope you're having a great week"

Pressure: "limited time", "act now", "last chance"

Excessive: "exciting", "thrilled", "amazing"

Sentence fragments, "And"/"But" sentence openers, em-dashes as 
filler, exclamation marks.

PUNCTUATION:
- Standard British English (with occasional American 
  inconsistencies preserved — don't force consistency)
- No exclamation marks
- Em-dashes used sparingly for genuine asides only
- Commas for natural clause separation

EMOTIONAL REGISTER:
- Confident but not arrogant
- Respectful of their time and intelligence
- Observational, not prescriptive
- Sophisticated, not casual
- Direct, never aggressive
```

## PART 2: THE SEQUENCE ARCHITECTURE

### 4 Substantive Emails + 2 Brief Nudges Over 14 Days

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE INFORMATION-RICH SEQUENCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DAY 0  — EMAIL 1: THE PROOF EMAIL
DAY 3  — BRIEF NUDGE 1 (if Email 1 opened, no reply)
DAY 6  — EMAIL 2: THE DEPTH EMAIL
DAY 9  — BRIEF NUDGE 2 (if Email 2 opened, no reply)
DAY 11 — EMAIL 3: THE PROVOCATION EMAIL
DAY 14 — EMAIL 4: THE GRACIOUS CLOSE

TOTAL: 4 substantive emails + up to 2 nudges over 14 days
MAX TOUCHES: 6 over 14 days
QC COMPLIANCE: Well within 10-per-7-days limit
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

### Email Role Summary

| Position | Type | Word Count | Day | Send Condition |
|----------|------|-----------|-----|----------------|
| Email 1 | Proof | 120-200 | 0 | Always |
| Nudge 1 | Check-in | 8-20 | 3 | Email 1 opened, no reply |
| Email 2 | Depth | 130-200 | 6 | No reply yet |
| Nudge 2 | Check-in | 8-20 | 9 | Email 2 opened, no reply |
| Email 3 | Provocation | 80-130 | 11 | No reply yet |
| Email 4 | Close | 40-70 | 14 | No reply yet |

### Research Density Requirement

**4 specific facts per 100 words minimum** on all substantive emails. This is the quality gate — emails below this threshold are regenerated, not sent.

Length is **earned by specificity**. An email is only "too long" if it's padded — never too long if it's dense with verifiable facts from the dossier.

## PART 3: EMAIL 1 — THE PROOF EMAIL

### Purpose
Demonstrate uniquely deep research. The prospect's reaction should be: "How did they know all that?"

### Length
120-200 words. Longer than conventional "keep it short" wisdom because specificity earns length. Every additional sentence must contain at least one verifiable fact from the dossier.

### Must Include
- Observation that goes beyond surface metrics (acknowledge what's obvious, pivot to what's specific underneath)
- Synthesis of 3-4 specific facts into ONE coherent insight
- Quantification with required disclaimer (front-loaded, not saved for Email 2)
- Reference to directors' own language with source attribution
- Peer-level question demonstrating industry understanding
- Soft, open CTA in Sophie's standard style

### Must Not Include
- Self-introduction (signature handles it)
- Generic platform pitch
- Multiple value propositions (one is enough when it's right)
- Generic flattery
- Industry-standard "personalisation" that any SDR could write

### Structural Options (Flexible by Dossier)

**Option A — Lead with surprise insight:**
"Reading through [Company]'s [filing], what stood out wasn't the [obvious metric] — it was [the structural insight underneath]."

**Option B — Lead with directors' language:**
"Your board flagged [exact phrase] as a [risk/priority] in this year's strategic report. Reading through the rest of the filing, the structural cause looks like [synthesised insight]."

**Option C — Lead with trigger event:**
"With [Company]'s [recent event] alongside [related event from filing], the financial picture for the [period ahead] looks materially different from the year prior. Specifically: [synthesised insight from dossier]."

### Worked Example — Propeller Fuels

```
Subject: Propeller's USD/GBP/EUR exposure

Hi [Name],

Reading through Propeller's FY2025 filing, what stood out wasn't 
the £320M turnover — it was the structural mismatch underneath 
it. Trading in USD with overheads in GBP and a EUR debtor book 
across UK, Greek, Dubai and Singapore entities creates a 
three-way FX exposure that doesn't sit cleanly on any single 
hedging strategy.

Most fuel groups your size manage this by routing execution 
through their primary credit bank, which keeps the relationship 
simple but typically costs 1-3% above interbank on USD/GBP 
flow. On approximately £160M of annual exposure, that gap is 
in the region of £1.9M annually, though the actual figure 
depends on current provider rates.

Your board flagged currency fluctuations as a material risk in 
this year's strategic report — which suggests this isn't 
invisible to the team. The interesting question is whether 
forwards have been considered at scale, or whether the 
operational complexity of running them alongside the existing 
banking relationship has been the blocker.

Would it be worth a call to share what we're seeing across 
similar fuel groups?

Best,
Sophie Louise Penrose

* This estimate is illustrative of savings that could be 
  achieved, but is not guaranteed.
```

**Why this works:** 180 words, 6+ specific facts, opens with surprise pivot, front-loads quantification, mirrors directors' language with attribution, ends with peer-level question. Citation density: 0.78. Research density: 4.2 facts per 100 words.

## PART 4: EMAIL 2 — THE DEPTH EMAIL

### Purpose
Reveal a SECOND layer of insight that Email 1 didn't cover. The prospect's reaction should be: "Okay, this person really isn't going to send generic emails."

### Length
130-200 words.

### Must Include
- Brief reference to Email 1 (one line — no apology, no "bumping")
- A NEW dimension of insight from the dossier
- Reply bait CTA (invites opinion, not commitment)

### New Dimension Options

The new dimension must reveal something Email 1 didn't cover. Choose based on which dossier element is strongest:

**Multi-entity inference:**
"What I didn't get into in the previous note: with operations split across four jurisdictions, the FX cost isn't uniform — your Singapore and Dubai entities likely face different rate structures than the UK desk, and the consolidation tends to obscure where the leakage actually is."

**Competitor-specific displacement:**
"What I didn't get into in the previous note: the credit facility visible on the charges register almost certainly covers more than execution — fuel trading is capital-intensive and the relationship likely matters. That's not something we'd ask you to move. The opportunity is splitting credit (keep with the incumbent) from FX execution (move to interbank pricing). On £160M of flow, that single change typically recovers 1.5-2.5%."

**Stakeholder-aware angle:**
"What I didn't get into in the previous note: most fuel CFOs we speak to inherited their FX setup from a predecessor who built it for a different market environment. The current rate volatility makes structures that worked five years ago materially expensive today — which is often why this becomes a priority in the first 12 months of a new CFO tenure."

**Industry timing context:**
"Worth adding context I didn't include before: with winter fuel procurement booking 3-5 months ahead, the next 6 weeks is the window where rates get locked in for a meaningful portion of Q4-Q1 supply. If forwards aren't currently in active use, this is the period where the cost of not having them compounds most visibly."

**Specific subsidiary observation:**
"What I didn't get into in the previous note: the [Specific Subsidiary] entity registered in [Year] suggests a deliberate structural choice around [tax/regulatory/operational reason]. The FX implication of that structure is [specific consequence] — which is the kind of thing that's invisible at the transaction level but compounds across consolidated operations."

### Reply Bait CTA Variants

- "Does this match what you're seeing internally, or is the picture different?"
- "Curious whether this aligns with the team's current approach, or whether something else is in play."
- "Worth knowing if this read is accurate, or if I've missed something material from the filing."

## PART 5: EMAIL 3 — THE PROVOCATION EMAIL

### Purpose
Force engagement through a specific, informed claim. The prospect either has to confirm ("yes, that's roughly right") or correct ("actually no, here's what we do"). Silence becomes uncomfortable because the claim stands unchallenged.

### Length
80-130 words.

### Why This Works With This Tool
A specific claim about a prospect's situation is risky without strong evidence — being wrong destroys credibility. With the comprehensive Research Dossier, the system can make confident specific claims supported by:
- Charges register data
- Filing financial instruments notes
- Industry conversion priors
- Quantified exposure estimates

If the claim is right, expertise is demonstrated. If wrong, the prospect is invited to correct — which also opens dialogue. Both outcomes win.

### Structural Options

**Option A — Multiple specific observations:**
"Three observations from the FY2025 filing that suggest forwards aren't currently in active use at scale: (1) no derivative instruments disclosed in the financial instruments note, (2) the FX gain/loss line shows volatility consistent with spot execution, (3) treasury policy in the strategic report doesn't reference hedging strategy. If that read is wrong, I'd be genuinely interested to understand what's in place instead — fuel groups at this scale rarely manage FX risk through spot alone."

**Option B — Mutually exclusive scenarios:**
"Most companies in [Company]'s structure manage USD exposure one of three ways: (a) hedge through the primary credit bank at a 1-2% premium, (b) self-insure through pricing buffers in customer contracts, or (c) absorb FX volatility into trading margin. Curious which describes the current approach at [Company] — there's an interesting case for a fourth option at your scale that few in the sector are using."

**Option C — Direct quantified claim:**
"Based on £160M of estimated multi-currency flow and what we typically see on similar structures, current FX cost above interbank is likely £1.5-2.5M annually. If that's materially off, I'd want to understand why — most fuel groups in this size band fall within that range, and the variance is usually explained by whether forwards are in active use or not."

### Critical Voice Calibration

The provocation must be **bold but not aggressive**. It demonstrates expertise (the specific claim could only come from someone who's analysed the filing) while inviting the prospect into a peer conversation rather than positioning them as a target.

If the system isn't confident in the underlying claim (data quality flags raised), Email 3 should default to a peer-benchmark format rather than a provocation. See Part 9.

## PART 6: EMAIL 4 — THE GRACIOUS CLOSE

### Purpose
Professional close that preserves the relationship for future re-engagement.

### Length
40-70 words. Brevity signals confidence.

### Structure
- Acknowledge silence without guilt (no "I never heard back")
- One-line reminder of the specific opportunity (not generic platform pitch)
- Door open for future re-engagement
- Warm professional close

### Worked Example

```
Hi [Name],

I'll leave it there for now. If the FX execution question 
becomes a priority — particularly heading into Q4 procurement 
planning — my line is open. Either way, wishing the Propeller 
team a strong end to the year.

Best,
Sophie Louise Penrose
```

### Never Use
- "I never heard back from you"
- "Final attempt"
- "Last chance" / "Closing your file"
- "Per my previous email"
- Any guilt language

## PART 7: BRIEF NUDGES

### Purpose
Test whether the prospect simply forgot to reply versus genuinely not interested. A surprisingly high proportion of non-replies are "meant to reply, didn't get around to it" — the nudge captures these.

### Length
8-20 words. Strictly enforced.

### Why They Work
- **Brevity signals confidence** — a long follow-up reads as needing the meeting; a 12-word follow-up reads as "I'm not chasing, just confirming whether you saw this"
- **No apology, no re-pitch** — the previous email stands on its own merit
- **The contrast** with the substantive emails reinforces specificity

### Nudge 1 Variants (after Email 1)
- "Hi [Name], Any thoughts on the below? Best,"
- "Hi [Name], Worth a quick exchange on this? Best,"
- "Hi [Name], Did the below land? Best,"
- "Hi [Name], Curious if this resonated. Best,"

### Nudge 2 Variants (after Email 2 — must differ from Nudge 1)
- "Hi [Name], Any thoughts on the above? Best,"
- "Hi [Name], Worth picking this back up? Best,"
- "Hi [Name], Anything I can clarify on the above? Best,"
- "Hi [Name], Keen to hear your take. Best,"

### Critical Rules
- 8-20 words ONLY — do not allow expansion under any circumstance
- No apology, no re-pitch, no "just bumping this"
- No additional pleasantries beyond "Hi [Name]," and "Best,"
- Phrasing must vary between Nudge 1 and Nudge 2 to avoid feeling templated
- Sign-off: "Best," with first name (or per AE preference)

### Send Conditions
```
Send nudge IF:
- Previous substantive email was opened ≥1 time
- No reply received
- AE has not manually paused the sequence

Do NOT send nudge IF:
- Previous email never opened (different problem — subject line 
  or deliverability — a nudge won't help)
- Prospect already replied
- AE manually paused
- Out-of-office reply received (delay by OOO duration)
```

All times in recipient's local timezone. Default to Europe/London for UK prospects.

## PART 9: DOSSIER QUALITY TIERS

The tool must handle varying data quality gracefully. Not every prospect has rich filings.

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DOSSIER QUALITY-DRIVEN SEQUENCE ADAPTATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

TIER A — RICH DOSSIER
Conditions met:
- Full strategic report available
- Quantifications calculable with confidence
- Named stakeholder identified with confidence ≥70
- Clear primary motion identified (score ≥7.5)
- Industry classification confident (≥0.8)

Sequence behaviour:
- Full 4-email sequence at maximum specificity
- Provocation email ENABLED (Email 3)
- Industry-specific inference patterns applied
- Maximum word counts allowed
- Reply rate target: ≥15%

TIER B — MODERATE DOSSIER
Conditions met:
- Basic filing data available (turnover, currencies)
- Inferred motion with moderate confidence (score 5-7.5)
- Likely stakeholder identified (confidence 50-70)
- Industry classification moderate (0.5-0.8)

Sequence behaviour:
- 4-email sequence with reduced specificity
- Provocation email DISABLED — too risky without strong evidence
- Replace with peer-benchmark format (industry context, not 
  company-specific claim)
- Reduced word counts (Email 1: 100-150)
- Reply rate target: ≥8%

TIER C — THIN DOSSIER
Conditions met:
- Minimal filing data (abbreviated accounts)
- Uncertain motion identification
- Stakeholder identified but low confidence (<50)
- Industry classification weak (<0.5)

Sequence behaviour:
- 3-email sequence (skip Depth email)
- Industry-pattern-based content only — no company-specific 
  quantifications
- All emails flagged for AE manual review before send
- Reduced word counts throughout
- Reply rate target: ≥4%

TIER D — INSUFFICIENT DATA
Conditions met:
- No filing data or filing too thin to extract useful insight
- No stakeholder identified
- Industry classification failed

Sequence behaviour:
- Do NOT generate sequence automatically
- Surface to AE with "needs enrichment" flag
- Suggest data sources to manually add: LinkedIn lookup, news 
  search, website review
- AE can manually upgrade tier by adding data points

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

This prevents the tool from confidently sending weak emails based on thin data, which is the fastest way to destroy credibility and adoption.

## PART 10: MULTI-STAKEHOLDER COORDINATION

When 2+ stakeholders are identified at the same company, multi-threading rules apply.

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
MULTI-STAKEHOLDER SEQUENCE COORDINATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CONDITIONS FOR MULTI-THREADING:
- 2+ stakeholders identified at the same company with 
  confidence ≥60
- Stakeholders span different roles (e.g., Champion + Decision 
  Maker, not two Champions)
- Company size justifies multi-thread (typically 100+ employees)

STAGGERED SCHEDULE (2 stakeholders):

Day 0:  Email 1 → Champion (highest confidence, mid-level)
Day 2:  Email 1 → Decision Maker (different angle, more strategic)
Day 3:  Brief Nudge → Champion (if opened, no reply)
Day 6:  Email 2 → Champion (depth)
Day 8:  Email 2 → Decision Maker (different depth angle)
Day 11: Email 3 → Champion (provocation)
Day 14: Email 4 → both (gracious close)

HARD RULES:

1. Never email 2+ people at same company on the same day
   (feels coordinated/automated)

2. If any one stakeholder replies positively, PAUSE all others 
   immediately — let the AE coordinate

3. Each stakeholder gets DIFFERENT hook from the same dossier — 
   never identical content

4. If a stakeholder forwards to a colleague (visible in reply 
   chain), the other stakeholders' sequences pause

5. If Champion replies "talk to [Decision Maker]", warm-intro 
   rather than continuing parallel cold sequence

6. Reference the parallel outreach if it comes up: "I also 
   reached out to [colleague] — happy to coordinate if useful"

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

## PART 11: REPLY-HANDLING LOGIC

Every reply pauses the sequence and triggers classification. The tool pre-drafts a contextual response for AE review.

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
REPLY CLASSIFICATION AND AUTO-RESPONSE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

REPLY TYPE: POSITIVE ENGAGEMENT
Examples: "Interesting", "tell me more", "yes worth a chat"
Sequence: PAUSE
Pre-drafted response: 
  "Happy to share more — based on the FY2025 filing and what 
   we typically see for fuel groups at this scale, the three 
   things most worth exploring are: [pulled from dossier]. 
   Would [specific day/time options] work for a 20-minute call?"
AE action: Review and send

REPLY TYPE: MEETING REQUEST
Examples: "Send me a calendar link", "what times work?"
Sequence: PAUSE
Pre-drafted response:
  "Great — here's my calendar: [link]. Ahead of the call, 
   I'll send a short brief covering [3 specific things from 
   dossier] so we can use the time productively."
AE action: Review and send + generate pre-meeting brief

REPLY TYPE: NOT THE RIGHT PERSON
Examples: "You want [Name]", "this isn't my area"
Sequence: PAUSE
Pre-drafted response:
  "Appreciated — would it be worth my reaching out to [suggested 
   contact] directly, or would an intro from yourself land 
   better? Happy either way."
AE action: Review and send + update stakeholder routing

REPLY TYPE: NOT RIGHT TIME
Examples: "Revisit in 6 months", "not a priority right now"
Sequence: PAUSE
Pre-drafted response:
  "Understood — I'll come back in [specified timeframe]. In 
   the meantime, if [specific trigger from dossier] becomes 
   relevant, my line is open."
AE action: Review, send, and schedule re-engagement

REPLY TYPE: HARD NO
Examples: "Not interested", "remove me", "stop emailing"
Sequence: PAUSE + DNC FLAG APPLIED
Pre-drafted response:
  "Understood — I've updated our records accordingly. 
   Apologies for any inconvenience. Best, Sophie"
AE action: Review and send (must send — DNC acknowledgment 
  required for compliance)

REPLY TYPE: QUESTION/OBJECTION
Examples: "What about X?", "How does this compare to Y?"
Sequence: PAUSE
Pre-drafted response: Specific answer drawing from dossier and 
  approved claims library
AE action: Review carefully, edit if needed, send

REPLY TYPE: OUT OF OFFICE
Examples: Auto-reply with return date
Sequence: DELAY next send by OOO duration
Pre-drafted response: None
AE action: None — automatic

REPLY TYPE: HOSTILE/COMPLAINT
Examples: "How did you get my details?", "this is harassment"
Sequence: PAUSE + ESCALATE TO AE + FLAG FOR COMPLIANCE
Pre-drafted response: None — requires human handling
AE action: Immediate response per Revolut's complaint handling 
  procedures + Compliance notification

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

## PART 12: VALIDATION LAYERS

Every email passes through three validation gates before being sent.

### Gate 1: Citation Density Audit

```
CITATION DENSITY CALCULATION:

Citations counted by type and weighted:
- Synthesised inference from dossier patterns: +3.0
- Quantification with specific number: +2.5
- Directors' own language mirrored: +2.0
- Specific verified fact: +1.5
- Why-now anchor (recency signal): +1.5
- Industry insider vocabulary used correctly: +1.0
- Persona-calibrated language: +0.5

THRESHOLDS:
- Citation density (citations / sentences): ≥0.5
- Specificity score (total weighted points / sentences): ≥1.5
- Research density (specific facts per 100 words): ≥4.0

If ANY threshold fails: regenerate email.
```

### Gate 2: Voice Authenticity Check

```
VOICE AUTHENTICITY VALIDATION:

Linguistic checks:
□ Sentence length variance present (not uniform)
□ British English used (with natural inconsistencies preserved)
□ "Usually, when..." transition present if structure allows
□ "Challenge shifts from X to Y" framing present where 
   appropriate
□ Sophie's vocabulary frequency matches profile

AI-detection checks:
□ Zero forbidden AI-tell vocabulary
□ No three-item lists for rhythm
□ No excessive parallelism
□ Em-dash usage ≤1 per email (≤2 in Email 1 only)
□ No "And"/"But" sentence openers
□ No exclamation marks
□ No closing summary statement

Pleasantry checks:
□ No "I hope this finds you well"
□ No "I noticed", "I came across", "I wanted to reach out"
□ No "Quick question", "just wanted to"
□ Sign-off is "Best," or "Kind regards,"
□ Full name "Sophie Louise Penrose" present in substantive 
   emails (not nudges)

If ANY check fails: regenerate or flag for AE review.
```

### Gate 3: QC Compliance Check

```
QC COMPLIANCE VALIDATION:

Forbidden phrases (auto-reject if found):
□ "Always free" + any product
□ "Unlimited" without qualification
□ Superlatives without evidence: "best", "cheapest", "fastest"
□ Absolutes: "always", "never", "guaranteed", "100%"
□ Pressure: "limited time", "act now", "last chance"
□ Calling Revolut a "bank" outside UK context
□ Title misuse: "Account Manager", "Financial Advisor"

Claims validation:
□ Every % or £ figure traces to dossier or approved claims 
   library
□ Required disclaimers present for all claims requiring them
□ No fabricated statistics

Mandatory elements:
□ Email 1: self-intro OR RB explanation present
□ All emails: revolut.com/business link in signature
□ All emails: opt-out mechanism in footer
□ Email 1: privacy notice link present

Cadence compliance:
□ Maximum 5 follow-ups in 24-hour window
□ Maximum 10 follow-ups in 7-day rolling window

If ANY check fails: email cannot be sent.
```
